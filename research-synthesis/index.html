CEOs don't care about learning — they care about workforce capability and competitive advantage. Learning is a means, not an end.

Key Research Finding — High Confidence

CEO Narrative: Build Capability, Not Headcount

The Problem You Feel

You can't find the talent you need. The senior people you want don't exist in the market — or they're too expensive. And when you do hire juniors, they leave before they're productive.

The Hidden Cost

Every failed hire costs 6-9 months of salary. Every vacancy costs productivity. And every departure takes institutional knowledge with it. You're running to stand still.

The Shift

The companies that will thrive aren't the ones who win the hiring war. They're the ones who build internal capability faster than the market can poach it.

CEO Psychographic Profile

Identity

Builder under siege

Fear

Company won't survive

Desire

Sustainable capability without headcount

Language

Growth, competitive advantage, efficiency

CEO Entry Hooks

  • 1. "What if you could stop competing for talent — and start creating it?"
  • 2. "The talent crisis isn't a hiring problem. It's a capability problem."
  • 3. "Your competitors can copy your product. They can't copy your people."
  • 4. "66% of Dutch businesses can't find staff. The 34% aren't recruiting better — they're developing faster."

You measure product quality. You measure sales pipeline. Why aren't you measuring learning velocity?

CEO Closing Hook

CHRO Narrative: From Proof Paralysis to Strategic Power

The Problem You Feel

You know L&D matters. You see the impact in retention, in engagement, in capability. But when the CFO asks for ROI, you have completion rates and satisfaction scores. That's not proof — that's hope.

The Trap

You're caught in a vicious cycle: without strategic budget, you can't prove strategic impact. Without strategic impact, you can't get strategic budget. Meanwhile, your seat at the strategy table is disappearing.

The Shift

The CHROs who break through don't wait for permission. They build proof systems that speak CFO language. They turn L&D from cost center to capability engine.

CHRO Psychographic Profile

Identity

Strategic voice trapped in admin role

Fear

Irrelevance

Desire

Seat at strategy table with proof

Language

Impact, alignment, business outcomes

CHRO Entry Hooks

  • 1. "What if you could walk into the CFO's office with numbers they actually care about?"
  • 2. "L&D's seat at the strategy table dropped 50% in two years. Are you part of the decline — or the comeback?"
  • 3. "The CFO doesn't hate L&D. They hate uncertainty. Give them certainty."
  • 4. "Stop defending your budget. Start proving your impact."

Only 4% of organizations can calculate L&D ROI. That's not a limitation — it's an opportunity.

CHRO Closing Hook

Interview Framework — Question Bank

CEO Interviews (6-8 target)

Section A: JTBD Validation

  • "Walk me through your biggest workforce challenge right now."
  • "What would you do with a fully-trained workforce that you can't do today?"
  • "When you think about competitors, what worries you most?"

Section B: Emotional Fractures

  • "Tell me about a time you felt stuck between hiring and developing."
  • "How confident are you that your company will thrive in 10 years?"
  • "What's your honest reaction when AI tools come up in strategy conversations?"

Section C: Meta-Theme Testing

  • "'Learning is the only sustainable competitive moat.' How does that land?"
  • "If you could have one thing competitors couldn't copy, what would it be?"

Section D: Channel & Offer

  • "Where do you consume business content? LinkedIn, podcasts, events?"
  • "If I offered you a free workforce capability assessment, would you take it?"

CHRO Interviews (4-6 target)

Section A: Strategic Pain

  • "Describe your relationship with the CFO when it comes to L&D budget."
  • "How often does L&D appear on the board agenda? What would change that?"
  • "What 'ammunition' do you wish you had for internal conversations?"

Section B: Proof Problem

  • "How do you currently prove L&D value? What's missing?"
  • "'Only 4% can calculate L&D ROI.' Where are you in that spectrum?"
  • "What would the CFO need to see to proactively support L&D budget?"

Section C: Coalition Building

  • "Who supports L&D internally? Who blocks?"
  • "How do you navigate the ondernemingsraad on training policies?"
  • "What would make line managers champion learning?"

Section D: Meta-Theme Testing

  • "'Learning is the only moat.' Does that framing help or hurt your internal case?"
  • "What language works with your CEO when discussing L&D?"
